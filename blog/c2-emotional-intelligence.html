Emotional Intelligence
Date: 17th April 2016
What learnings did you take from the chapter?

The most important leaning for me from the chapter is that EQ is trainable. Why this is important to me is because I feel that I need to really work on improving my EQ and the faith/ the conviction that it is trainable is hence, very important.

I have to work on developing high resolution awareness of developing awareness as soon as they arise. And for this I have to practice Mindfulness.
- I shall start by paying more attention to my thoughts and emotions.
- I will consciously try to keep my mind in the present in all my day to day activities.
- If I have any distractions then I will try and recover from them promptly.
- Develop emotional stabilisation by trying to reflect on doing it before I do it.

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?

I tried meditation and it was not easy for me. I wanted mind brain to just be as is where is and not to think about anything but as you can figure out it didn’t work well for me... I kept on going back to thoughts that I haven’t spoken to mom and dad today, what is that I’ll make for dinner, and how did my sisters exam go today….

Then I thought instead if aiming for the blank I’ll start by focussing on my breathing, and to make sure that I don’t waver I started counting my breathing and kept my closed eye focused on my nose (if that’s even possible... ). I think I must have counted till about 70 and then I don’t know why I stopped counting but I was in a vacuum and sometime after that my timer went off and that’s when I felt that it went of pretty fast and I would have liked to be in that state for some more time.

I am going to try and practice this every day from now on…

And yes for people who find it hard, try counting your breathe … it surely helps.
