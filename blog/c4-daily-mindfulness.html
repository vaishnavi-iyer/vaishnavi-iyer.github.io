Meditation Process
Date: 25th April 2016
What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

I am a very open person in the sense that I have always been talking to people about my day. From my childhood, I am used to coming home and talking my heart to my mom, my dad, my sister and sometimes my brother, and after my marriage my husband. All these people know everyone I pretty much interact with as I discuss with them everything that goes on during the day.

So mindfulness talking is not a new concept for me… I like speaking my heart out to my family and then my family is the same. At 26 yrs, working full time, studying for almost 30 hours and also doing work at home, I still manage to talk to my parents every day for at least 30 mins and same with my husband gives me a lot f opportunity to this. This is just heart to heart talking for.

As far as their experience goes, I think they like it that’s why they haven’t complained yet. I believe this brings us close.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I really liked the idea of mindfulness in activity. I need to learn how to appreciate simple things more and not to take anything for granted and for that I should learn to bring mindfulness in moments.

Also, Lightness and joy in meditation is also something I really liked. When I thought about happy moments it gave me a different kind of peace altogether and practise of coming back to the neutral state, I believe, will offer my mind a good training.

Any other musings?

I am also contemplating the idea of brain dump on paper more as it will give me a way of letting things out of my system without me having to discuss with anyone. It doesn’t mean that I don’t enjoy talking to my family but more on the lines of trying to understand how it works for me.
